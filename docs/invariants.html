Special Relativity - Collected Invariants

 speed limit c
 squared space-time interval s2
 space-time volumes Ω
 time-order for events having a time-like separation
 electric charge e
 mass m (rest mass, if you prefer)
 cross-sectional area of a beam of photons
   perpendicular to the direction of motion (McKinley)
 E^2 - H^2 
 E dot H
 curvature of space-time R
 degree of polarization P (Landau)
 degree of circular polarization A=ξ1 (Landau)
 degree of linear polarization l=root(ξ1^2+ξ2^2) (Landau)
 geometric outline of an object, as seen in a camera (Terrell)
   the apparent size is not preserved; just the shape
 collision cross-section, the probability of collision between two beams of particles
   (or the num collisions per unit time, unit volume)
 relativistic action integrals
 trace and determinant of second-rank tensors
 luminosity of radiation from 
   any isotropic emitter
   an accelerated charge (dipole radiation, low speed!)
