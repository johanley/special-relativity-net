Doppler Effect

The Doppler effect applies to any periodic phenomena - anything that happens at regular intervals. The Doppler effect is a result of two things:

  • changes in travel-time from the signal source to the signal detector
  • time dilation for the moving signal source

Here, the two detectors receive an incoming periodic signal from the emitter, the source of signals. The signals are emitted both to the right and left. The white dots represent the ticking of wristwatch time along each history. As the emitter increases its speed, you can see the effect of time dilation in the increasing spaces between the ticks.

As you can see, the reception of signals:

  • gets piled up on Detector 2, which the source is approaching; that detector sees the signal frequency as increasing.
  • gets spread out on Detector 1, which the source is receding from; that detector sees the signal frequency as decreasing.
